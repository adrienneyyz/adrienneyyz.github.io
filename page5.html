Are Chiropractors A Good Resource for Nutrition Advice?
Dr. Jen Garven D.C., Dr. Carlee Aitchison D.C. - Contributor

Many patients aren’t aware of the extensive training in biochemistry and nutrition that chiropractors receive.  Chiropractors help manage conditions such as diabetes, obesity, coronary artery disease, and issues affecting the musculoskeletal system; all of which diet and proper nutrition can play an important role in the reduction of symptoms. Chiropractors often assist athletes with performance optimization, or recovery from injury in which nutrition can also play a major role. Despite all of this, a chiropractor generally isn’t the first person people think of when they seek nutritional advice, but will all the misinformation readily available these days it may be wise to clarify with a professional.

A recent 2013 survey of chiropractors’ use of nutrition in private practice indicated that 81% of chiropractors incorporated nutritional advice into their practice, and most felt that comfortable addressing all nutritional concerns1. Diet and nutrition is undeniable important in the pursuit of health. So if you need nutritional counseling, if you want to know the most recent literature on a specific diet you are thinking of trying, or questions about supplements you can rest easy knowing your chiropractor can play a valuable and competent role in this area of your health.

1. Smith, D.L, Spillman, D.M. A survey of chiropractors’ use of nutrition in private practice. Journal of Chiropractic Humanities. 2013; 10 (93-98). doi:10.1016/S1556-3499(13)60135-7 (Accessed January 5, 2015)
